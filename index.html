MENTAL HEALTH

Mental health problems are more frequent in women than in men.
Prevalence, influencing and risk factors, symptomatology,
and course of the disorder is different in women than in men.
For example, mood or anxiety disorders are
more common in women but they are affected by
schizophrenic psychoses later in life.
Premenstrual dysphoric disorder, and perimenopause-related depression
happen only to women.
And certain mental disorders such as bipolar disorder
and schizophrenia though common to both men
and women are experienced differently and
progress differently in women.
